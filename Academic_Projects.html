Visualizing Photocatalysis

One the more rewarding parts of my PhD work was developing code to visualize the processes I was simulating. DFT calculations are just numbers on a screen so I enjoyed writing scripts to convert those to more digestible forms.

-Charge Transfer-

When a plasmonic nanoparticle is excited by an external field the generated surface plasmon my transfer charge to an adsorbed molecule by hot carriers. Visualizing areas of charge depletion and accumulation is useful for understanding this process. I wrote a serious of scripts to extract the charge data from a RT-TDDFT calculation and write it in format readable by VMD. VMD can then render the system frame by frame to create a movie like the following.

This shows a Cu nanoparticle with a Ru atom in the center of the top layer and a nitrogen molecule adsorbed on the surface. As an electric field is applied we can observed where charge moves in the system (red indicates negative charge accumulation and blue is positive).

-Near-field Enhancement-

In addition to hot-carrier generation, plasmonic nanoparticles are known to enhance the local electric field at the surface on the material. I wrote some code to visualize this enhance field.

This shows the enhanced electric field on a 55-atom Au nanoparticle at ~40 fs into the simulation when an external electric field is applied. The script creates planar slices starting from the bottom of the unit cell moving upward through the nanoparticle. Brighter spots indicate regions of a stronger field, showing it is larger on the sides and edges of the nanoparticle.

In this case I show the time-varying enhanced field at a fixed plane on the surface of the nanoparticle. Again the field is observed to be largest on the sides and edges. These insights may be useful for the rational design of plasmonic photocatalysts and only arise by being able to visualize the structures.
